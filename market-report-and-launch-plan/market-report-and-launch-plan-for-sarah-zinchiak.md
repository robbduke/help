## 1. **Executive Overview**

### Provider Profile

* License: Certified Family Nurse Practitioner (CRNP), licensed in Maryland
* Practice Model: Solo telehealth behavioral health practice
* Current Patient Base: 300+ established private-pay patients (ages 6–70)
* Service Area: Calvert County, Southern Maryland; extended reach across Southern Maryland region and beyond via telehealth
* Specialization: ADHD evaluation, medication management, and behavioral health medication management
* Modality: 100% telehealth
* Patient Encounter Volume: Currently 75–80 visits per week private-pay; scalable with payer enrollment
* Business Status: Incorporated, Tax ID established, NPI and EIN in place

### Strategic Objective

This FNP is transitioning from a 100% private-pay model to a hybrid revenue model that incorporates insurance-based reimbursement while preserving existing patient relationships and revenue. The goal is to:

1. Enroll with major federal, Medicaid, and commercial payers serving Southern Maryland and the broader region
2. Establish a realistic revenue ramp that accounts for credentialing timelines and patient eligibility
3. Project sustainable insurance revenue alongside maintained private-pay practice
4. Identify key risks and mitigation strategies specific to FNP behavioral health credentialing

### Market Position

The FNP operates in a market with:

* High federal employee concentration due to proximity to Andrews Air Force Base and Patuxent River Naval Air Station
* Significant Medicaid and Medicare populations across Calvert, Charles, and St. Mary's counties
* Strong private-pay patient base already established and paying out-of-pocket
* Growing demand for behavioral health services, particularly ADHD diagnosis and medication management
* Limited competition for telehealth-based FNP-led ADHD care in the region

### Critical FNP-Specific Considerations

#### 1. Type 2 NPI & Practice Entity Setup

The FNP should establish a Type 2 NPI for the practice entity (separate from personal Type 1 NPI). This approach:

* Allows billing under the practice tax ID
* Maintains clean payor credentialing records
* Simplifies payer enrollment if practice grows
* Is strongly recommended even for solo practitioners

Status: ✓ Already in place per consultation

#### 2. License Type & Scope of Practice Verification

<mark style="background-color:yellow;">FNPs in Maryland practice under a collaborative agreement with a physician per state law, though the scope for behavioral health and ADHD management is well-established. Payers will verify:</mark>

* <mark style="background-color:yellow;">CRNP Maryland license</mark>
* <mark style="background-color:yellow;">Collaborative physician agreement (required)</mark>
* <mark style="background-color:yellow;">DEA registration (FNPs prescribe controlled substances; DEA number required for ADHD medication management)</mark>
* <mark style="background-color:yellow;">Malpractice insurance (behavioral health specialty required)</mark>

<mark style="background-color:yellow;">Action Required: Ensure collaborative physician agreement is in place and documented for credentialing submission.</mark>

#### 3. Behavioral Health Network Placement

Some payers (especially national carriers like UnitedHealthcare, Aetna) may route FNP behavioral health through a contracted behavioral health network rather than general medical panels. Examples:

* UnitedHealthcare: Behavioral health may flow through Optum Behavioral Health or a regional network
* Aetna: Separate credentialing pathway for behavioral health providers
* Medicaid MCOs: Behavioral health often managed separately from medical services

<mark style="background-color:yellow;">Implication: Enrollment timelines may differ between medical and behavioral health panels, and the FNP may not appear in medical directories but will appear in behavioral health directories.</mark>

#### 4. Telehealth Authorization

All proposed payers cover telehealth for behavioral health services. However:

* <mark style="background-color:yellow;">Some payers require initial visit in person; most allow full telehealth for follow-ups</mark>
* <mark style="background-color:yellow;">A few payers (Medicaid MCOs in some states) require periodic in-person visits; Maryland Medicaid MCOs generally do not</mark>
* FNP should maintain documentation of patient state of residence (telehealth only permitted to patients in Maryland currently)

Assumption: All payers in this plan cover full telehealth for behavioral health and ADHD management.
